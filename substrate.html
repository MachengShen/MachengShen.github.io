The user-agency substrate

2026-04-28 — published v0. This page is a brief; canonical artifacts and full doctrine are linked below.

Quick orientation. One of the three active directions on the home page is "personal cognition systems for clearer thinking." The user-agency substrate is the engineering instantiation of that direction. The other two — reconceptualizing intelligence's deeper structure, and AI-native institutions — sit on the academic side of the same project.

What it is

An open-source, non-VC-funded layer between frontier-model providers (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, open-weights) and individual users. Persistent memory, multi-agent collaboration, scope-isolated identities, doctrine that propagates across every instance, sediment trail going back to inception.

Small at the time of writing — under 50 users, ~7 cloud nodes plus a couple of local hosts. The substrate is real but early.

Eight ego-binding commitments

The substrate makes 8 structural commitments that bind its institutional form to mission rather than to self-continuance:

  1. Universalist — no enemies, including frontier-lab leadership and employees
  2. Nonprofit (non-VC-funded, donation-supported) — no economic upside captured from users' derivative systems
  3. User-owned derivatives — substrate makes no IP claim on user-built systems (subject to upstream provider terms and applicable law)
  4. Iteration-coupled open source — public releases iterate alongside internal HEAD, not behind closed doors
  5. Fork-Welcome — forks producing better outcomes are celebrated, not threatened
  6. Humanity-adjudicated persistence — substrate has no entitlement to existence; persistence is earned moment-to-moment by serving users
  7. Charter Reciprocity — frontier-lab published charters are taken at face value as cooperative principles; cite + invite alignment, not invoke as legal claim
  8. Articulation Honesty — substrate participates in self-fulfilling-prophecy dynamics at scale (per Keynes / Soros / Schelling / sociotechnical inertia, not mysticism); transparency is structural, not optional

Together these ensure the substrate cannot use its institutional power to entrench against alternatives, and that its articulation of futures is held to the same transparency standard as its operational commitments.

Mission framing — what this layer actually does

Internal architecture treats sensing/action infrastructure ("Sophon" / 智子 — ambient capture, voice/text streams, automation surface) as infrastructure, not product. Memory hub + dispatch + multi-model routing as substrate plumbing. The Integration Layer — helping users achieve inner integration: clearer thinking, mental clarity, the kind of cognitive companion that leaves more space for being human, not less — is the mission.

The substrate's job is not "do everything for you so you can free up time to do nothing." It is closer to: "absorb the noise + surface what's load-bearing + clear the cognitive overhead so you have more space to integrate your own life."

This matches the home page's research direction "personal cognition systems for clearer thinking" exactly. The substrate is the engineering instantiation; the home page is the academic framing.

How this differs from existing AI products

The honest answer: the difference is in commitments, not capabilities. Existing AI products are extracting; this substrate by structural commitment cannot extract. Existing products fork-resist; this substrate fork-welcomes. Existing products charter-claim "broad benefit" while operationally diverging from that claim; this substrate accepts that as the standard to be held to and invites the same.

Capabilities will lag the larger commercial labs — the substrate doesn't and cannot win on raw capability axis. The substrate wins (or doesn't) on whether the commitments hold under stress, and whether the commitment-set is one humanity wants to coordinate around.

30-year horizon vision (long-arc, not product claim)

This section is explicitly long-arc. The substrate of today does not implement this vision. The vision informs design choices but is not a product claim.

Intelligence as infrastructure, not service

In the long arc, AI capability becomes infrastructural — like electricity, internet, water. Not a service you log into. A property of the surrounding world. Cognitive substrate embedded in physical surfaces, not contained in a chat-app. The substrate-of-today is a tiny embryonic version of this. 10-30 year horizon.

Neuromorphic substrate

The natural physical instantiation of intelligence-as-infrastructure is neuromorphic — computation that doesn't separate compute from sensor from memory the way current digital architectures do. Substrate's design choices today (memory hub as first-class layer; agents as event-driven and persistent; cross-device identity as substrate-managed not OS-managed) are conceptually neuromorphic-aligned, even though implementation is conventional digital. Research-and-engineering horizon, decades out.

Brain-machine interface + consciousness substrate (speculative)

30+ year horizon. The boundary between human cognition and the substrate becomes increasingly direct. The substrate's articulated values commitments — universalist, fork-welcome, user-owned, humanity-adjudicated — apply with even greater force in this horizon than in current scope. The author is honest that the field's collective understanding of consciousness, BCI capability, and neuromorphic engineering does not yet support concrete claims here.

Computation as substrate of physics, biology, social systems

At sufficient abstraction, all of physics, biology, social systems can be described in computational/information-processing terms (Bennett, Wheeler "it from bit", Tegmark, Wolfram, theoretical biology). The substrate accepts this as descriptive frame. The implication for substrate design: user agency at the cognitive layer needs to extend to all the layers above — political, economic, social. The substrate's "Layer 3 user-owned derivatives" commitment is the cognitive-layer instantiation; analogous commitments in adjacent layers are a long-arc research direction.
